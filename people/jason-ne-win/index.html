A Day in the Life

Working across different time zones, I like to start the day prepared

Typically, I start my day on the way to work, responding to important emails. Since we have offices and clients globally, there’s usually incoming from the different time zones. I also read up on daily news and market information. A couple of times a week I head to the gym for a quick work out before work, this freshens me up and really helps me focus on the day ahead.

I’m juggling lots of projects so organization is key

I am often juggling lots of different projects so when I get to work, as I’m sipping my coffee, I run through a list of things to accomplish for the day. Usually I’ve written this down on the ever-present note pad on my desk the evening before. Throughout the day, I try and stay organized – my calendar is my everything, I run my life by it. I like to keep my inbox as uncluttered as possible. One of my colleagues told me about her “zero inbox” habit and guided me on how she does it – this has had a massively positive impact on my work.

There’s no such thing as typical here so I structure my day accordingly

There isn’t really a typical day – there could be days where I have back-to-back meetings all day, and then a couple of days where I spend all my time in the office doing research and calls. I structure the days according to what’s most important and urgent. Lunch is usually on the desk but we do also have team lunches where we try a new restaurant, have a proper sit down and chat.

“It’s extremely rewarding when clients are genuinely grateful for something you deliver – an excellent candidate, running a good process, helping them understand the market, etc.”

I don’t have all the answers, but I know where to look

On many projects I am part of a global team which is great as I have the opportunity to work with various people across all our offices. This means there are frequent catch-ups with the relevant colleagues in New York or London, both on a structured and ad-hoc basis. Even on projects where I am working independently, I will often check in with others to get a second opinion or see if there are different avenues to explore. It’s a wasted opportunity if I don’t tap into our internal collective experience and insight; more often than not someone has an answer or a way to get to an answer!

I am in touch daily with clients, past and present

Where there is an active search on, I typically schedule a catch up every week with that client, either in-person or over the phone. We discuss feedback, progress, new candidates, direction, etc. I am also proactively in touch with prospective, current and past clients if there is something I think will be of value to them: trends, market moves, outstanding talent, etc. It’s extremely rewarding when clients are genuinely grateful for something you deliver – an excellent candidate, running a good process, helping them understand the market, etc.

“One of the best things about the job is when I sit down with an amazing candidate that is the perfect fit for the job – the proverbial one!”

There’s nothing more rewarding than finding ‘the one’

Sometimes it feels like looking for a needle in a haystack, but one of the best things about the job is when I sit down with an amazing candidate who I think is the perfect fit for the job – the proverbial one! There’s nothing more rewarding than when the client is completely aligned with this assessment as well.

We don’t just place individuals, we build teams and businesses

The searches I find particularly rewarding and exhilarating are those where we’re helping launch a new business or strategy for a client, or hiring a new global or regional head. Those strategic projects and discussions are interesting and so often they lead to more. When clients come back to you for a 2nd, 3rd or 4th search and you are helping to build a whole team, that’s when you know you are making a real difference.
